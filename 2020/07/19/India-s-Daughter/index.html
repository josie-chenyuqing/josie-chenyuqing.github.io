India's Daughter

India’s Daughter
On December 16, 2012, Jyoti Singh Pandey, an Indian medical intern, mistakenly boarded a bus that was not on duty at 8.30pm and was later beaten and gang-raped by six male passengers with iron bars. She suffered severe wounds to her abdomen, intestines and genitals after being attacked with iron bars. On December 29, Jodie died due to unsuccessful rescue efforts, and it took 13 days for her to die.
India’s Daughter, a BBC documentary on the subject, was to be released on March 8, 2015, international Women’s Day. In an unexpected twist, Indian authorities recently controversially banned the film from the country, citing its sensitive content as potentially disruptive.
After watching it, I cried miserably. Sometimes I find that we are powerless in front of many things. No matter how powerful the power of ordinary people is, even if it is a parade, there is no way to bring the victims back true justice, not to mention an apology.
A dying girl who had been gang-raped and beaten lay naked in the wilderness, surrounded by more than 30 men, with no one to help her. No one came forward when the police came with sheets and water and asked for help.
Jodie was fortunate to have been born in a family where there was no son preference and she was about to graduate to become a doctor.
Who ruined it all?
It was the rapist who beat her, the innocent bystanders, the rapist’s defense lawyer, the Indian authorities who banned the documentary, all the dirty, sexually-minded people in the world.
“It takes two to tango,” rapist Mukesh Singh said. A decent girl doesn’t hang out at 9 o ‘clock at night. Girls are responsible for rape.” He even said that when a girl is raped, there should be no resistance. She should have been raped quietly. Then when they were done they would throw her out of the car and give the boy only a beating. Defense lawyers say only flowers placed in temples can be respected.
The scariest part of the story is that she didn’t get on the bus alone, and her boyfriend was also badly beaten.
