Yale Open Course:Philosophy-Death

Yale Open Course: Philosophy - Death

Having recently become interested in philosophy again (yes, for the second time), I went online to find the Open Course on Philosophy at Yale university and attended the first two lectures. These are a little difficult for me to understand, so I’ve summarized the previous part that I can understand and made a summary.

The popular view is that death damages our bodies, but since our souls are immaterial, they can survive death. Hope for a soul. Death sucks. Possibility of immortality. Because life is so precious, it is so shameful to waste it that people should not attempt suicide.
But it turns out that’s wrong, there’s no soul, and immortality is not a good thing. Fear of death is a normal response. Suicide can also be justified and morally justified.
The ultimate goal: to make students think independently and look at death objectively
PS: Try not to take religious views into account

  1. The nature of people
    (1) What is a person made of? What am I?
    The second theory holds that a person is composed of a body and a soul. The body and soul interact with each other. Once the body dies, the soul will lose contact but still exist
    According to physicalism, a person is just made up of bodies, and a person is a material entity
    Idealism holds that man has only his soul, and the real world is imagined by the soul.
    Can I survive my death?
    Rebuttal: The question itself is false and confuses concepts

Jay Rosenberg: When a person dies, that’s the end of life, so the question becomes, “Can a person continue to live after the end of life?” “The answer, of course, is” no “. There can be no new life after death.

Death, blood stops flowing, the brain is deprived of oxygen, the cells can’t repair the wound, the vital cells fail, the body dies.

1, What is the idea,or What is the concept of surviving?

Philosophy is really interesting, because I found out that the professor is really doing a deep analysis of a few common problems, which seems boring and keeps repeating a point, but is actually a way of thinking. It’s a pity that I couldn’t finish the course because of language problems, but I hope I can have access to similar courses in the future.
