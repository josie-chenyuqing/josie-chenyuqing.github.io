City 24 hours

城市二十四小时

【郑州】【武汉】【深圳】

这是三座生活节奏非常快的城市。

郑州是个中转站，它是中国的十字路口，有中国最大编组站，中国最繁忙的车站以及全球最大医院。

在凌晨四点左右，火车站便人满为患，来自各地的人们由这里出发，涌向其他所有的省份，显而易见，在这里，你可以看见整个中国。而截止至次日凌晨两点，全球最大的医院郑州大学附属第一医院才刚刚落下今日的帷幕。

站长与医生，都是平凡而忙碌的职业，在熙熙攘攘的人群中帮助他人，是他们的职责以及毕生坚持的信仰。

每个城市都有新旧交替或共同存在的现象。

郑州有最大的iPhone生产线，也有古老的国棉四厂。年轻人走向四海八荒的人才市场谋一份前程，老人们则悠然自得的待在过去的时光里，守着一份属于自己的安逸。

（但我没有搞懂为什么要花那么长时间去讲豫剧，豫剧确实是当地的文化特色，但强调有很多小孩子去学我就不太明白了）

而市井气和快速发展也同样是武汉灵魂的一部分，提到武汉，人们首先想到的是热干面和光谷。在老街吃完早饭后，一批又一批的年轻人进入市场，为资本而奋斗。无数人站在失败者的肩上往上爬。

深圳就更不用提了，最年轻的城市，发展速度极快，所有人都遵循超过996的作息时间，数十乃至上百亿的资金在公司之间流动。每一次会谈都是在成功与被淘汰之间博弈。

尽管前路坎坷，仍有无数新深圳人来到这个逐梦之地，让深圳不得不建了世界上数量最多的摩天大楼以及填了相当于两个澳门那么大的海域。

【成都】【厦门】

这两座城市相比较而言会慢节奏一点。

成都是著名的小吃圣地，连街边都有便宜而便民的露天茶馆。外地人来成都里，去宽窄巷子感受他们心中的成都，而成都人更愿意去周边的农家乐，做一个田园诗人，乐得清闲。但他们共同的割舍不掉的就是火锅。他们平均每人可以等待的极限时间是3.5个小时，真是可歌可泣。

对比成都浓重的城市味儿，厦门的人们最常去的则是秀美的鼓浪屿。鼓浪屿与世隔绝，虽然与厦门岛仅仅相隔600米，岛上却缺失很多必需品，得靠推板车的年轻人拉回来。

午后，岛上的音乐家

这两座城市都兴盛书店和酒吧，文艺青年不爱回家，于是就去书吧里畅读一夜，或是去酒吧来首民谣。这也就是为什么这儿的年轻人平均睡觉时间比上面三座繁忙的城市更晚的原因吧。

这部纪录片没有把焦点放在城市本生上，反倒是多了不少生活的烟火气，描写了非常多的城中村，把人生的本质体现的淋漓尽致。

Classification standard of endangered animals [1] : safe: although there is a threat, it is not serious at present. “Low concern” does not mean that the species has received little attention, but that the threat is not serious enough to be of undue concern. For example: Taiwan blue magpie, Wolf. Near danger: When a taxon does not meet the criteria of extremely dangerous, endangered, or vulnerable, but is close to meeting or likely to meet the threat level after a period of time. For example, small heads sleep sharks, rabbits. Vulnerable: There may be a relatively high threat of extinction in the medium term. Examples: ring-tailed lemurs, great white sharks, polar bears. Endangered: Wild populations face a high probability of extinction in the near future. For example: the blue whale, the panda. Critically endangered: The risk of imminent extinction of wild populations is very high. Examples: milu deer, Taiwan salmon. Extinction in the wild: a taxon is considered extinct in the wild when it lives only in cultivated, captive conditions, or as a naturalized population (or population) living far from its former habitat. For example: Dromedary, Taiwan sika deer. Extinction: A taxon is considered extinct if there is reason to suspect that the last individual of the taxon is dead. For example: Thylacine, dodo bird, Taiwan clouded leopard.

 
2. Survival status of endangered animals
In recent centuries, thousands of species of wild animals and plants have become extinct in the world, among which 150 species of birds, 95 species of beasts and 80 species of amphibians and reptiles have become extinct since the 16th century. Statistics from the International Union for conservation of Nature show that 5 percent to 20 percent of vertebrate and tree species are now threatened with extinction. Of the more than 100 vertebrates in danger of extinction worldwide, about 14 are the result of natural evolution. The rest is due to human factors. Many wild animals in China are endangered, such as red-crowned cranes, Chinese alligators, giant pandas and South China tigers. So it is urgent to protect habitats and restore damaged ecosystems. According to reliable research, the world disappears one kind of animal about every hour and one kind of creature about every minute.

  1. Reasons for the emergence of endangered animals
    1) Chinese demand for game is increasing. In the south, where the game market is very dense, 53 snake species are brought to the table in China and three quarters of the freshwater turtle population has sharply decreased. From 2007 to 2016, 209 pangolin smuggling cases were detected in China, including 2,405 live pangolins and 11,419 dead ones.

2) Parts of wild animals that can be made into medicine or fur coats, such as ivory and antlers, are so valuable that even under strict laws, a steady flow of poachers go to major reserves to kill rare animals.

3) Pollution caused by human production and continuous reclamation of new land will destroy the habitat of wild animals, thus leading to the emergence of endangered animals.
Such as:
(1) The star-nosed mole feeds on worms and earthworms, which have certain requirements on soil fertility. Because human beings do not carry out garbage classification, they also throw electronic waste randomly, which leads to the gradual spread of toxic substances in the land. Earthworms, worms and other worms cannot survive, while the number of star-nose-mole has decreased sharply in recent years due to the lack of food.
(2) The survival of the baiji is also under threat. Garbage in the middle and lower reaches of the Yangtze River is piled up at will on the river bank, and garbage from ships is discharged into the river at will. In recent years, construction garbage and chemical garbage are dumped along with the waste liquid, making the Yangtze River water seriously polluted and threatening the survival of the Baiji dolphin.
(3) The hawksbill turtle population has declined sharply. Coral reefs, where these creatures live, have been decimated by human waste and ozone depletion
4). Endangered animals like China’s giant panda, with low fertility and harsh living conditions (bamboo is a staple food), are already in decline. The natural enemies of endangered animals either increase in Numbers due to overproduction by humans, or they have less food (plants) to survive on
The South China tiger is the most endangered of all tiger species. Living in south-central China, it is estimated that there are only 20-30 south China tigers left in the wild in the world. There are now 47 south China tigers in 18 Chinese zoos around the world. Zoos can only breed in captivity
