Critically endangered: The risk of imminent extinction of wild populations is very high. Examples: milu deer, Taiwan salmon. Strange consumer psychology

Strange consumer psychology

  1. The higher the price, the better to sell – Vanity effect (Egret Effect)

In 1950 Harvey Leibenstein, an American economist, published the vanity effect and its corresponding conformity effect.

Rich people generally do not like the public to imitate their consumption behavior, because they want to be different when they buy goods. To put it simply, the vanity effect is the tendency to pursue high-grade goods.

Specific manifestations of vanity effect:

  1. Buy high-end products quickly when they first come on the market.
  2. Stop buying once its market share reaches a level that the general public can consume.

The higher the goods are, the more they are consumed by individuals, the more obvious the vanity effect is. If it is not mainly for personal consumption, and the goods are purchased for external display, even if the price is high, the higher the price, the more the demand will increase the herd effect.

If it is believed that vanity effect is more likely to occur in the target market, marketing policies:

  1. Pay more attention to the lifetime value of a product than its market share. That is to say, in the choice of market strategy, focus on maintaining the original customers, because if only focus on the immediate interests and focus on market expansion and ignore the original customers, one day even the original customers will leave. Moreover, at some point, the marketing strategy must not only stop expanding new users, it must also do demarketing to prevent the influx of new users.
  2. Absolutely avoid price competition. Cutting prices creates two problems. The general public, which is sensitive to price, will buy the product after the price has been lowered
    (1) Reduce the scarcity of commodities, leading to the departure of existing customers
    (2) The deterioration of commodity quality.
  3. As a late entrant, try to avoid business promotions such as “me too”. Because markets with a vanity effect seem to have good yields and few competitors.
    When a new competitor enters the market, the market itself may collapse. The participation of new competitors will increase consumption, but the original customers may choose to leave the market because of the vanity effect. The original business had already enjoyed high profits, so it could recoup its investment costs, while the new competitors could only suffer huge losses.
  1. Big Spending Saves the Economy – Paradox of Thrift
    If you consume more than your income, you have no way to save, and you end up living in poverty. So we work hard to be rich, and sometimes we become stingy, trying to save more money.
    This is true for individuals, but bad for the economy as a whole.
    Can the country really become richer if all its citizens become stingy? The answer is no, especially in a bad economy. Let’s see why.

Once the economy goes into recession, people will save more and consume less because they are worried about the future. But if everyone consumes less, business sales will fall and inventories will increase. As a result, companies can only reduce production and employment, and their employees’ income will decrease. Worries about the future will intensify, people will become more determined to spend less and save more, and the economy as a whole will be trapped in a downward spiral.
This leads to the paradox that saving is reasonable from an individual’s point of view, but not economically as a whole. This is called the Paradox of Thrift. If businesses cannot increase their consumption, the government should also step in to increase government spending by offering preferential terms for enterprises to invest and make efforts to attract foreign investors and tourists to increase investment and consumption. Only in this way can a virtuous circle be formed and the economy eventually get better.

  1. Free is not always good – Free Economics
    “There is no such thing as a free lunch” was Milton Friedman, the famous economist who won the Nobel Prize for economics in 1976
    Some information may seem free at first glance, but in fact it doesn’t mean free at all. Or it can be interpreted as some kind of lure behind free, or it can be interpreted as not expecting something free at no cost.

Gradually, however, the case for true free is gaining ground. Just as bad money drives out good, the trend towards free eviction fees is reinforced. The Economist, a British newspaper, looked ahead to 2008 and named the trend “freeconomics”, combining the words “free” and “economics” to form a new word.

Look around us and you’ll find a lot of free marketing, including the free newspaper in front of the subway station. Free newspapers, which once threatened dailies and sports newspapers, have large readership and are highly profitable from advertising. Less popular freeshees would be pushed out of business because they could not generate enough advertising revenue, so that the surviving freeshees could gradually occupy more favorable positions. The richer the company, the more likely it is to survive the free competition. Therefore, consumers may also face losses caused by monopoly.
However, another drawback of free markets is the waste of resources. Free newspapers, for example, waste a lot of newsprint because they are given away free to consumers. A system of mass production and mass consumption can lead to an overuse of energy and lead to an acceleration of global warming.

Super carrier Reagan

Great engineering campaign, the title is the highest praise for the aircraft carrier! America’s technological prowess is truly admirable. Super carrier Ronald Reagan is the most advanced nuclear-powered aircraft carrier and single weapon in the world. It is also the first aircraft carrier commissioned by the United States in the 21st century. It operates in international waters and in American territory, with aircraft, weapons and a fine navy of 5, 000, ready to be there when it is needed (often for long-distance transport).The Reagan is the uss nimitz-class aircraft carrier 9, built by the United States in the late 1990s, and named after Ronald Reagan, the 40th President of the United States. Reagan, the first former US President to name a navy ship while still alive, died a year after the carrier began service. Since then, the carrier has become a symbol of the President, following Reagan’s motto of “strength for peace”. This is indeed a viable idea. Powerful combat weapons patrolling the sea, especially in conflict zones, can enhance the country’s deterrence and highlight America’s military strength. The ship is 332.85 meters long, 78.34 meters wide, has a deck area of 18,000 square meters, displaces 97,000 tons of water, rises 20 stories above the waterline, stands as high as the Empire State Building and can reach speeds of 35 miles per hour. The ship carries 85 aircraft of various types and can accommodate 6,000 crew members. Equipped with two nuclear reactor engines, the Reagan can run continuously for 20 years without refueling. The ship has enough food to feed 6,000 soldiers for three months. The service period is 50 years. Equipped with all the latest technology, the carrier can house 85 fighter jets at a cost of $4.5 billion. The Ronald Reagan is not a zero anymore, it’s a great project. As long as authorized by the President of the United States, personnel can deploy the carrier’s battle group anywhere in the world.

Along with the high technology of the Reagan is the caution of the personnel involved, whose work is both a glory and a constraint. This is also the most dangerous working environment. The soldiers are trained in the most rigorous way, and no small mistakes that might lead to disaster are allowed. At the heart of the operation was the 400 crew on deck, who had to follow the flow of the jet engines, and the first lesson was to put up with the noise. Others, such as aircraft controllers, need to coordinate their work, fold up the aircraft, put it in the right place, and stretch the limited space to the limit. At the same time, maintenance and oil is also an essential link, work is extremely busy, often need to do two things at once, both sides have to grasp, always tracking the dynamics of each aircraft. Frequent landings lead to busy ground crews shuttling back and forth at a controlled time. The master maneuvers this most delicate instrument, like a perfect ballet. In order to ensure the safety of personnel and distinguish the work of various departments, the soldiers changed into rainbow clothing to ensure bright colors, so as to quickly identify the crew.

Without the longest runway, the crew USES wind and propellers to help the plane take off, allowing it to accelerate for a short time. Landing was even riskier, reaching speeds of up to 150 miles per hour before landing, and landing on a mother ship that swayed with the waves was even more difficult, with constant and deadly pressure. At this point, partners need to cooperate, measure the Angle of the landing, to ensure that the blocking line hook, this is the end of a landing.

The following is the question of supplies. The Reagan is powered by two nuclear reactors at the bottom, which work with turbine engines and provide enough power to power a city of 100,000 people. The equipment can run for 20 years, but the weekly replenishment (supplies, fuel, parts) on board is essential. The ships must maintain a constant speed and a safe distance of 160 feet for three to six hours. First, a rope was thrown to transport pallets of supplies, then fuel was added through a hose. The safety team is particularly important here, keeping an eye on the process in case one of the crew’s clothes gets hooked or the locks snap.

To sum up, we can accuse the United States of inhumanity in other places, but the United States is the undisputed king of technology, as evidenced by the super carrier Ronald Reagan and the supporting fighter system. The hope is that the carrier, like its motto, will exist to protect peace, not to threaten other countries by force.

Five young men. A pair of brothers, a mature and steady brother and a timid brother, a female nurse who has just passed the examination, a young woman who works in a bar and a Jew.
The two brothers joined the army, and the nurse went to the field hospital. A few good friends then celebrated the night before they left and agreed to get together again at Christmas. They were laughing and having fun, defying curfews. They feel fearless, too optimistic about war and too simplistic about human nature.
One of the great little details of the miniseries is that it marks the distance from Berlin before it gets anywhere. This corresponds to the young people saying “see you in Berlin at Christmas” before saying goodbye.
On July 10, 1941, the battle of Leningrad began.
First, the brothers, who went to opposite extremes.
Brother will ham is the main narrator of the film, which opens as a second lieutenant in greyhound company. He was a typical German, fearless and eager to give his country everything he had. Full of passion and energy, he is always the center of the crowd and a strategic leader. In his mind, war makes men mature.
His strength came from his belief that Hitler could lead Germany to a brighter future. So at first he looked down upon his brother who wanted to escape the war.
But as the battle lines stretched, from 1941 to 1943, will began to wonder what he was fighting for. He discovered the nature of war, he knew it was wrong. He could not answer to the germans or the soviets, nor could he confide in his soldiers. In the end, he became the deserter he had most hated.
Then will found the meaning of life – to live. He can’t be a hero, and he doesn’t want to be a pig in a slaughterhouse.
In contrast to his emotional brother, brother Fred hamm is so rational that he is probably the most lucid person in the show. Because he reads so many books, he looks further than his friends. He read the French poet rambo. Rimbaud is a religious icon whose poetry is full of arcanism and who has no contempt for hypocrisy. Rimbaud also participated in the Paris society and wrote an ode to the uprising on March 18, 871. Fried inherited the poet’s vulnerability, and he understood the fascist plot, but could not dissuade anyone around him.
He hated war, but perhaps he hated his own weakness even more.
So he was reluctant to shoot in the early days of the Soviet invasion.
But Fried is very adaptable. His brother will have doubts, will give up because of compassion, and he won’t. He abandons compassion, and in order to survive, he chooses to become a machine that can only carry out the orders of his superiors.
This confirms what he said before the war, that war brings out the bad in people.
He thought a Nazi lieutenant colonel had killed a jewish girl and was crying.
He has fallen.
Maybe at some point he wanted to return to the light. But germans, and even his parents, seem to regard his return from injury as a humiliation. Under pressure, he returned to the battlefield.
In 1945, scattered German soldiers on the eastern front began to return from various fronts, and friedham and several national stormtroopers met the soviets on their way home.
A few hot-blooded young men wanted to die for their country.
“You are children,” said friedham finally. “the russians will send you home.”
Then he ran into a Russian bullet and used his body to tell the young men behind him that the war was over.
A good soldier is weak most of the time, but strong occasionally.
At last he was truly brave, and then he collapsed on the eve of peace.
The reason why he left the birth to his children was that his character had not changed from beginning to end. Laughably, the elder brother has always been the mature brother, and the younger brother has always been the weak and kind brother.
Will fled because he had a sense of responsibility. He knew that it was wrong to attack the Soviet union, but his soldiers would be condemned if he stopped. His brother became the executioner not because he was sensible, but because he was afraid, he did not want to die, can only to adapt to the harsh environment, then he lived a muddle, death in his release.
Maybe it is a kind of atonement, when he did not save the little girl of the Soviet union, finally found the result here.
Then there was Sally, the war nurse. She was the youngest, eager to serve her country, and she had the childlike innocence of a child who silently loved will.
Sometimes, however, the child’s unconscious actions are even more brutal.
At first she was hopeful, compassionate, and distressed by the soldiers’ injuries. But her “flower of evil” also blossomed during the war, and she became a qualified field nurse, turning on jewish women and even betraying Soviet girls who had helped her.
Her fear and doubt in the face of the war is very small, I see from her is not vicious, but a weak girl with layers of cocoon to protect themselves. Pathetic and pathetic. She represents the vast majority of ordinary people, muddled with the leader’s pace, not to think why, also dare not resist.
Fortunately, the human nature of the good, finally returned to the good, in order to sonny gave up the retreat, finally survived.
And Greta.
Greta is a contradictory individual. She is so beautiful that her friends think she will be a star. But the cruel war could not bear an innocent and useless woman. She traded the only thing she had, beauty, for the chance for her jewish boyfriend to escape, for an empty life.
Her love affair with a young Jew, victor, was Platonic, not carnal. She really loved him, across race and across national faith. She lives both fully and confusedly.
On April 11, 1945, she was executed for defeatism.
Did not see the dawn, no lover drunk smoked kiss.
She died with a pale skin. At least in the end she is clean, like a ray of moonlight, according to the broken heart.
I can’t tell if she’s wrong. Chastity and flesh are sometimes worthless in the face of life. In giving her a chance, she may yet seduce for the sake of her future with her lover
On May 8, 1945, Germany surrendered.
In the devastated streets, victor, a Jew, was free to walk, and he would no longer be humiliated by racial discrimination.
The family lost their sewing shop at kristallnacht and life took a nosedib. He lost almost everything in the war, which shows the impotence of small groups in the face of large forces. He suffered most from a broken heart. Victor knew that he and the germans are not different, but he had to rely on his girlfriend’s body for escape, he wandered all the way, save also be saved. He was the one who truly saw the warmth of the world.
He returned to the prewar bar and lifted the lid. He didn’t play the piano because the beautiful girl who danced was dead. He lost the love of his life.
Without rock music and baked cakes, without tearful hugs and kisses, the three men who were alive approached each other with distancing precaution.
They toasted their dead old friends and brothers.
The wounds of the war are hard to heal, and every young face in the camera has read all the sorrows of the world, becoming vicissitudes and confusion. World war ii is over, and then what? Where do they go from here?
I don’t know if they will meet again. They might get together and talk about their wishful thinking, but maybe they won’t, because it will only remind them of those four bloody years.
After I read it, I felt very painful. Sometimes my personal fate paled before the war. It’s not anybody who’s wrong. It’s the war. It’s everybody.
May there be no war in the world, and may all people make the right decisions when the chips are down.
